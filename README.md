# About Me

I am an Applied Data Scientist and ML Systems Engineer based in Kolkata, India, with approximately four years of industry experience building production-grade machine learning systems. My work focuses on designing and deploying machine learning solutions that directly support operational and strategic decision-making across large-scale industrial and commercial environments. 

Rather than focusing solely on modeling, my work typically spans the entire lifecycle of machine learning systems. This includes data ingestion, feature engineering pipelines, model training infrastructure, evaluation frameworks, inference services, and monitoring systems. I am particularly interested in building ML architectures that remain reliable under real-world constraints such as evolving data distributions, feature growth, and operational requirements.

Across my work, the central goal has been to translate machine learning models into systems that generate measurable business outcomes such as revenue growth, procurement savings, improved operational efficiency, and enhanced decision support.

# Professional Experience

Over the past several years, I have worked on machine learning systems across the retail, energy infrastructure, and commodity procurement domains. These systems have ranged from forecasting pipelines and anomaly detection models to recommendation engines and market intelligence systems.

Earlier in my career, I worked on multiple ML systems within the Adani ecosystem, where my responsibilities included designing and implementing machine learning solutions supporting procurement strategy, industrial monitoring, and retail analytics. The systems developed during this period included price intelligence models for global solar component markets, forecasting systems for commodity metals used in power transmission, anomaly detection pipelines for solar power plants, and recommendation engines serving large-scale retail networks. 

Currently, I work as an Associate Projects professional within Cognizant's AI and Machine Learning practice, where my work focuses on designing reusable ML architectures and production pipelines for multiple industry domains including manufacturing, utilities, and retail. In addition to system development, part of my role involves mentoring teams on production-grade machine learning engineering practices and helping standardize ML system design across projects.

# Capabilities and Technical Focus

My technical work sits at the intersection of machine learning modeling and system engineering.

On the modeling side, my experience includes forecasting systems, gradient boosting models, representation learning approaches, anomaly detection using autoencoders, recommendation systems, clustering techniques such as HDBSCAN and KMeans, and statistical modeling approaches used for market and operational forecasting. 

On the engineering side, I focus on building reproducible and maintainable ML systems. This includes implementing experiment tracking, model versioning, feature pipelines, and monitoring workflows using tools such as MLflow and cloud-based machine learning platforms. I have experience deploying ML systems using infrastructure components including Vertex AI, Cloud Run, and Databricks environments. 

In addition to traditional ML systems, I have also worked with LLM-based systems and generative AI architectures using tools such as Azure OpenAI, LangChain, and LangGraph to build applications that combine structured data pipelines with language-model-driven reasoning and automation.

A significant part of my work has involved applying machine learning to domains where analytical systems directly influence operational and financial decisions.

# Current Areas of Exploration

Beyond production ML systems, I am also interested in exploring the transition from traditional predictive models toward more autonomous analytical systems.

One example of this direction is my work on the Autonomous Metal system, which explores how machine learning forecasts, structured data signals, and contextual information can be combined with language models to simulate the analytical workflow of a commodity market analyst. The goal of this work is to move beyond standalone predictive models toward systems capable of producing explainable analytical outputs that resemble human analyst reasoning. 

Another area of interest involves building more stable and interpretable segmentation systems using representation learning and clustering techniques that remain reliable across retraining cycles and evolving datasets.
